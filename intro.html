This is some information regarding the impact of fast fashion trends:

What is Fast Fashion?

Quick Summary:
  • Fast fashion refers to inexpensive clothing that is produced quickly to keep up with constantly changing trends. Brands release new styles very frequently, often inspired by what is popular on social media, celebrities, or influencers. Because of this, trends change extremely fast, encouraging people to keep buying new clothes to stay “in style.” As a result, many people purchase more clothing than they actually need and only wear items a few times before moving on to something new. These clothes are often made with lower-quality materials so they are cheaper to produce, which means they wear out quickly and are thrown away sooner. Overall, fast fashion focuses on producing large amounts of clothing at a low cost rather than creating durable, long-lasting pieces.

Why is it a Problem? enviorment:

Read more

Fast fashion has a major impact on the environment. Producing clothing requires large amounts of water, especially for materials like cotton. In addition, the dyes and chemicals used in manufacturing often pollute rivers and oceans. A significant amount of clothing ends up in landfills each year, where it can take a long time to break down. Many fast fashion items are made from synthetic fabrics like polyester, which release microplastics into the environment when washed. These microplastics can harm marine life and enter the food chain.

workers

Fast fashion also affects the people who make the clothing. Many workers in the industry are paid very low wages and work in unsafe or unhealthy conditions. In some cases, workers do not have basic rights or protections. There are also situations where child labor is involved, especially in countries where labor laws are not strongly enforced. Overall, the fast fashion system often prioritizes low production costs over fair treatment of workers.

Olympia:

Quick Summary:
  • Olympia replaces real human connection by setting an unrealistic and unreachable standard
  • Nathaniel becomes so obsessed that he neglects his other relationships

What She Represents

Read more

Olympia represents and idealized, yet unreal partner that sets standards so high that it simply is not achievable. Nathaniel wants to attain this perfection, when in reality he should be focusing on connection.

Obsession with Olympia

Read more

Olympia is docile, agreeable, and beautiful, but she is a robot and has no true feelings for herself. Nathaniel becomes obsessed with her and neglects all other aspects of his life. "Clara's image had been wiped clean from his mind" (Hoffmann 112) and "his mother, Lothario, all had vanished from memory" (Hoffmann 117).

Rejection of his other Relationships

Read more

Nathaniel prefers the “predictability” and “passiveness” of Olympia over the real, flawed human relationships (like with Clara). Nathaniel became so obsessed with Olympia that he neglected to foster his true relationships, abandoning his letter to Clara and shutting off his family from memory. Through this example, Hoffmann critiques the way technology can distance us from authentic social bonds.

What's Real and What's Not

Another prevalent symbol in The Sandman is the telescope, which alters Nathaniel's perception of reality. This is supported because throughout the book, he struggles to differentiate which of his relationships are genuine and which are not. The telescope influences what he sees, making him narrow-minded and clouding his perception. This is evident in how he looks through the telescope to find Olympia, where he becomes enamored and believes it to be a genuine relationship.

Narrowing of Perception

Read more

When Nahtaniel looked at Clara through it, he was again deceived and feared she was like Olympia, and grew paranoid, ruining his genuine relationship because of this suspicion as he tries to kill her. This is shown in the book, when Nathaniel sees Clara through the glass of the telescope, "a spasm shuddered through him; pale as death, he stared at Clara […] and with terrible force he seized Clara and tried to throw her off the tower" (Hoffmann 123). This affected him as he is unable to perceive genuine emotions, and therefore his suspicion involuntarily sabotages his real connections.
